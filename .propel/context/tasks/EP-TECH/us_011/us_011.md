## Story Title
   * Operational Runbooks & Incident Playbooks

## Description
  * As a SRE/Platform Engineer, I want authored operational runbooks and incident playbooks for deployments, restores, key compromise and escalation with testable procedures and defined RTO/RPO, so that on-call responders and operators can consistently and quickly restore service, contain security incidents, and demonstrate recoverability and compliance.

## Acceptance Criteria
  * Given the platform repository exists, When I open the "runbooks/operational" directory, Then I find versioned runbooks for: Deployment (canary/blue-green), Database Restore (PITR & snapshot), Key Compromise (rotate/revoke keys), and Incident Escalation (severity matrix and contacts), each with clear owner, last-reviewed date, and change log.
  * Given a documented restore runbook, When a simulated restore is executed following the runbook, Then the restore completes within the documented RTO and data restored meets the documented RPO and the test records (time-to-restore, data-consistency checks, issues encountered) are added to the runbook audit section.
  * Given a documented key compromise playbook, When the playbook is executed in a tabletop or automated drill, Then secrets/material are rotated per the steps, dependent services are validated for functionality, and post-incident verification checks pass (no failed auth attempts due to rotation) and the action log is captured.
  * Given a deployment runbook, When an automated or manual rollback is triggered per the runbook (smoke test fail after deployment), Then the rollback completes and the service health returns to pre-deploy baseline with documented verification steps and a post-mortem checklist is created/linked.
  * Given the incident escalation playbook, When an incident is declared (severity S1/S2), Then on-call contacts are paged as per escalation matrix, escalation time thresholds are enforced, required stakeholders are notified, the incident runbook is followed (containment, mitigation, recovery), and a post-incident report template is populated with timelines and RTO/RPO impact.
  * Given runbooks are stored in the platform repo, When a user requests access, Then runbooks are accessible through the documented path with read permissions for responders and write/approve workflow limited to runbook owners and the platform team; secrets in runbooks are referenced by KMS/secret path (no plaintext secrets).
  * Given a quarterly review cadence, When the scheduled review date occurs, Then runbook owners confirm validity, runbook tests (tabletop or live drills) are scheduled or executed, and the review outcome is recorded (pass/fail/needs-update) in the runbook metadata.

## Edge Cases
   * What happens when a restore fails due to partial backup corruption?
     - The runbook includes immediate branching steps: attempt alternate backup snapshot, escalate to DB tier on-call, initiate forensic capture of corrupted backup, and if neither snapshot meets RPO, declare DR escalation and follow the mitigation/communication plan. All attempts and decisions are logged in the runbook audit and communicated to stakeholders.
   * How does system handle KMS or secret store unavailability during key rotation?
     - The key compromise playbook includes an offline fallback: use pre-approved emergency keys stored in an offline sealed artifact accessible with multi-person approval, failover directives to isolate affected services, and instructions to restore KMS connectivity (or perform emergency rotation using signed scripts). Post-event, rotate emergency keys and capture sequence in runbook history.
   * What happens when on-call paging or primary contact info is unreachable?
     - The escalation playbook defines secondary/tertiary contacts, escalation via alternate channels (SMS, phone, email, chat ops), and a policy for automatic escalation to the next-level manager after X minutes. If no human can be reached, the runbook instructs automated mitigation scripts (traffic reroute, scaled-down mode) to preserve availability until manual intervention can resume.
